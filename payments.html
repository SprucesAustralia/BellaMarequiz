Training Pay Structure

When a new cleaner is being trained, the pay for the first two rooms they clean together with their trainer is allocated as follows:

  • The Trainer receives the full standard room rate for those two rooms.
  • The Trainee receives one-third (1/3) of the standard room rate for those two rooms.

After the first two training rooms, pay is split according to the standard 'Splitting Pay' rules described below.

Splitting Pay Between Cleaners

Often, departure or deep cleans are assigned to teams of two or sometimes three cleaners to ensure efficiency.

  • If two cleaners are assigned to a room, the total room rate (based on type, day, and fix-up status) is split evenly, with each cleaner receiving half (1/2) of the total rate.
  • If three cleaners are assigned to a room, the total room rate is split evenly, with each cleaner receiving one-third (1/3) of the total rate.

While the pay *per room* is lower when splitting, working in a team allows cleaners to complete rooms faster, potentially cleaning more rooms within the same shift, thus balancing out the earnings potential.

Criteria for 'Fix Ups Required' Rate Reduction

The $3 reduction ('Fix Ups Required' rate) applies to standard departure and weekly service cleans when specific, basic housekeeping tasks are missed or not completed to standard upon inspection, requiring a return or correction. This is focused on general clean quality, not deep cleaning issues. Examples include:

  • Missed dusting on easily reachable surfaces (tables, sills, furniture)
  • Floors not properly vacuumed or mopped (visible debris, streaks)
  • Mirrors or glass surfaces left streaky
  • Bathroom details missed (toilet bowl not clean, soap scum on shower screen, vanity not wiped)
  • Kitchen surfaces not wiped down (benchtop crumbs, sink not clean)
  • Rubbish bins not emptied
  • Items not replaced correctly (e.g., towels folded improperly, amenities missing)

Exclusions: You will not be placed on the lower rate for call-backs related to deep cleaning tasks, such as:

  • Removing stubborn wall marks or scuffs
  • Carpet stain removal
  • Air conditioner filter/vent cleaning
  • Oven or rangehood deep cleaning
  • Tasks typically associated with a bond clean

Additional Work

From time to time, we may request extra work outside of standard cleans (e.g., spring cleaning tasks, specific small maintenance support). This will be paid in blocks of 15 minutes, each block paying $10 AUD.

Payment Method

Please note, we pay via ABN (Australian Business Number), not TFN (Tax File Number), as this work falls under legislation for contracted services.
