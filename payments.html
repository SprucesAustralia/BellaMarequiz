Training Pay Structure

When a new cleaner is being trained, the pay for the first two rooms they clean together with their trainer is allocated as follows:

  • The Trainer receives the full standard room rate for those two rooms.
  • The Trainee receives one-third (1/3) of the standard room rate for those two rooms.

After the first two training rooms, pay is split according to the standard 'Splitting Pay' rules described below.

Splitting Pay Between Cleaners

Often, departure or deep cleans are assigned to teams of two or sometimes three cleaners to ensure efficiency.

  • If two cleaners are assigned to a room, the total room rate (based on type, day, and fix-up status for Departure/Weekly, or the fixed rate for Deep Cleans) is split evenly, with each cleaner receiving half (1/2) of the total rate.
  • If three cleaners are assigned to a room, the total room rate is split evenly, with each cleaner receiving one-third (1/3) of the total rate.

While the pay *per room* is lower when splitting, working in a team allows cleaners to complete rooms faster, potentially cleaning more rooms within the same shift, thus balancing out the earnings potential.

Quality Standards & Rate Adjustments (Departure/Weekly Cleans)

The $3 rate adjustment applies only to standard Departure and Weekly Service cleans based on inspection quality. Deep cleans have inherent quality expectations and are not subject to this specific $3 adjustment.

Triggers $3 Reduction ('Fix Ups Required' Rate) Does NOT Trigger $3 Reduction (Considered Deep Clean / Non-Standard / External Factor)
Basic Housekeeping Issues:
  • Missed dusting on reachable surfaces (tables, sills)
  • Floors not properly vacuumed/mopped (visible debris, streaks)
  • Streaky mirrors or glass
  • Bathroom details missed (toilet bowl, shower screen scum, vanity not wiped)
  • Kitchen surfaces not wiped (benchtop crumbs, sink unclean)
  • Rubbish bins not emptied
  • Items not replaced correctly (towels, amenities)
 Deep Clean / Maintenance Issues:
  • Stubborn wall marks or scuffs
  • Carpet stain removal
  • Air conditioner filter/vent cleaning
  • Oven or rangehood deep cleaning
  • Extensive window/track cleaning
  • Mould removal (beyond minor surface spots)
  • Tasks typically part of a bond clean
  • Issues requiring maintenance personnel (e.g., blocked drain, blown bulb)
  • Excessively dirty apartment beyond normal guest usage (may qualify for extra time/pay upon approval)

Pay Calculation Examples

Scenario 1: Standard Departure Clean

Details: 2 cleaners, 2 Bedroom apartment, Weekday, clean passed inspection (No Fix Ups).

Rate Used: 2 Bed, No Fix Ups Rate = $63

Calculation: $63 / 2 cleaners = $31.50 per cleaner

Scenario 2: Standard Departure Clean

Details: 3 cleaners, 3 Bedroom apartment, Weekday, clean required minor corrections (Fix Ups Required).

Rate Used: 3 Bed, Fix Ups Required Rate = $82

Calculation: $82 / 3 cleaners = $27.33 per cleaner (approx.)

Scenario 3: Standard Departure Clean

Details: 1 cleaner, 2 Bed Split apartment, Sunday, clean required minor corrections (Fix Ups Required).

Rate Used: 2 Bed Split, Fix Ups Required Rate (Sun/PH) = $72

Calculation: $72 / 1 cleaner = $72 per cleaner

Scenario 4: Deep Clean

Details: 2 cleaners, 3 Bed Villa Deep Clean.

Rate Used: Fixed Total Rate for 3 Bed Villa Deep Clean = $272

Calculation: $272 / 2 cleaners = $136.00 per cleaner

(Note: The $3 fix-up adjustment does not apply to deep clean rates)

Additional Work

From time to time, we may request extra work outside of standard cleans (e.g., spring cleaning tasks, specific small maintenance support). This will be paid in blocks of 15 minutes, each block paying $10 AUD.

Payment Method

Please note, we pay via ABN (Australian Business Number), not TFN (Tax File Number), as this work falls under legislation for contracted services.
