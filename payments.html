Am I a Regular or a Casual?

Regular Contractor

  • Works every week
  • Does 3 or more days per week
  • i.e. 12+ shifts in the past 4 weeks

Casual Contractor

  • Does not work every week, or
  • Averages fewer than 3 days per week over the past 4 weeks
  • i.e. fewer than 12 shifts in the past 4 weeks

Your status is reviewed on a rolling 4-week basis. If your availability and shifts increase consistently, you move to Regular rates. If they drop, you move to Casual rates.

Understanding the $10 Bonus

This is the most important thing to understand about your pay.

The $10 is a BONUS you EARN

It is not a penalty or deduction. Your base rate is the lower amount. When the room goes through with no issues flagged, you earn an extra $10 on top as a reward for perfect work.

Important context: The hotel hired us to clean rooms to their standard. That means, in a normal day, hotel staff should not need to double-check our work – the expectation is that every room is ready for guests straight after we finish.

If hotel staff do check a room and find something, it only happens because our standard wasn’t met. That’s the moment the bonus is no longer earned – not because they’re “inspecting” everyone, but because they had to step in and fix what should have been right.

What is a “fix-up”?

A fix-up happens when the hotel inspects your room and finds something that needs to be corrected – for example, a missed spot, streaky glass, or an unclean area. Someone (often you, another cleaner, or hotel staff) has to go back and fix it.

Why does the bonus get removed? The bonus rewards rooms that are done right the first time. If someone has to go back, the room was not perfect – so the bonus is not earned. You still receive your full base rate.

Why does the $10 even exist?

Every fix-up triggers a chain of unpaid admin work (inspection, photos, review, comms, re-check). The $10 bonus rewards rooms done right the first time – because that’s what saves everyone the hassle.

Basic Issues (Bonus NOT Earned)

These are things within your control. If found, the $10 bonus is not earned for that room.

  • ● Missed dusting on reachable surfaces
  • ● Floors not vacuumed/mopped properly
  • ● Streaky mirrors or glass
  • ● Bathroom details (toilet, soap scum)
  • ● Shower mould not cleaned
  • ● Kitchen crumbs or unclean sink
  • ● Rubbish bins not emptied
  • ● Dishwasher not emptied / dishes not put away

Acceptable Issues (Bonus Still Earned)

These are outside your control or require specialist work. They will NOT affect your bonus.

  • ● Stubborn wall marks or scuffs
  • ● Carpet stain removal
  • ● Deep oven/rangehood cleaning
  • ● Structural mould (walls, ceilings – maintenance issue)
  • ● Maintenance issues (drains, bulbs, locks)
  • ● Excessively dirty rooms (beyond normal guest use)

Training Pay Structure

When training a new team member:

  • Trainer: Gets 100% rate for first 2 rooms.
  • Trainee: Gets 1/3 rate for first 2 rooms.
  • After room 2: Normal team split applies (50/50 for 2 people).

Example: 7 rooms with 2 people – Rooms 1–2 trainer gets 100%, trainee gets 33%. Rooms 3–7 split 50/50.

Team Splits

Total room rate is divided evenly by cleaners:

  • Solo: 100% of rate – you keep everything.
  • 2 Cleaners: 50% each (room takes half the time).
  • 3 Cleaners: 33% each (room takes 1/3 the time).

More people = faster rooms = more rooms per shift. Hourly earnings stay similar.

How Room Allocation Works

4. Keep your availability updated in the app so we know when you can work.

5. Check with management before assuming you are working on any given day.
